General Overview

Salem, Oregon, is the state capital located in the Willamette Valley. Known for its rich history, Salem features several historic buildings and sites, including the Oregon State Capitol. The city is surrounded by beautiful natural areas, such as parks and nearby vineyards in the renowned wine country of the Willamette Valley. Salem also offers a vibrant cultural scene, with events like the Salem Art Fair and Festival drawing visitors each year. Additionally, its proximity to both the coast and the mountains makes it an ideal spot for outdoor enthusiasts.

Interesting Facts

  • 25% of the population of this city is under the age of 15.
  • This area, and all of Willamette Valley, is famous for its tap water.
  • Native Americans once called the surrounding area Chim-i-ki-ti, which means "meeting place."
  • Salem's first two capital buildings burned down.
  • Cherries are grown around here, leading to its nickname of "Cherry City."
